What Scapia is building

Scapia is building India's first travel-native financial platform — not just a credit card, but an end-to-end ecosystem where you earn, plan, book, and spend all in one app. The card is the entry point; the booking platform, experiences marketplace, and Scapia Store are the flywheel.

Core bet: Gen Z and Millennials want travel to feel like a lifestyle, not a transaction. Premium travel cards charge ₹5,000–10,000/year. Scapia gives zero fees, zero forex, and unlimited lounge access — and funds it through booking margins and ecosystem GMV.

Even as Scapia raised the lounge spend threshold to ₹20K/month in early 2026, the core proposition remains powerful for frequent travellers. The next product layer must convert passive coin earners into active, loyal travellers.

Core product surface

  • 10% Scapia Coins on regular spends (≈2% value, 5 coins = ₹1) · 20% on in-app travel bookings (≈4% value)
  • Zero forex markup — Visa + RuPay dual card
  • Unlimited domestic lounge access (₹20K/month spend)
  • Airport Privileges — dine, shop, spa, lounge on departure

  • Scapia Store — travel gear with 20% coin earn + redeem

Scapia Travel Goals

Turning passive coin accumulation into an active, visible savings journey — so every coffee, commute, and grocery run feels like a step closer to the next trip.

Feature Proposal Retention + Engagement AI-First 6–8 Week Build

The Problem

Scapia's coin system is technically simple — earn 10%, redeem for travel — but emotionally invisible. Users accumulate coins passively with no sense of progress, no destination, and no narrative. The result: coins feel abstract, the app feels like a statement viewer between trips, and when it's time to redeem, users don't know if they have "enough" or what they can actually do.

"Still trying to understand the coins system… now with the ₹20K threshold, it feels even harder to justify."

— Reddit + App Store sentiment, early 2026

The trust signal is clear: users are watching. If Scapia doesn't give coins a narrative before they feel pointless, the comparison to CRED becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. The fix isn't the coin math — it's giving coins a destination.

User Insight

Scapia's core user — a 24–32 year old urban professional — already mentally budgets for travel. They think "I want to do Bali in December" or "I want to hit Goa with the team in September." What they don't have is a tool that connects today's chai purchase to that trip. Every other financial product makes saving feel like sacrifice. Scapia has a rare chance to make it feel like anticipation.

Rathinamurthy's post specifically called out AI-first thinking as a hiring requirement. This feature is exactly where AI earns its place — not as a gimmick, but as the engine that makes a personalised goal feel smart, alive, and achievable.

Proposed Solution — Scapia Travel Goals

A lightweight in-app feature that lets users set a travel goal — a destination, a date, a rough budget — and then watches their coin balance grow toward it in real time. Three core flows:

Step 01

Set Your Goal

Pick a destination (or let AI suggest one based on spend patterns + season). Set a travel month. Scapia estimates the coin cost of flights + hotels from your city.

Step 02

Watch It Build

A persistent "goal card" on the home screen shows % toward the trip. Every transaction nudges the bar. "Your Goa weekend is 34% funded." Coins are no longer abstract.

Step 03

Smart Nudges

AI sends contextual prompts — "₹3,200 away from your Bali flight. Dining out this weekend? Earn 2x if you pay with Scapia." Spend becomes intentional, not passive.

Where AI Does the Heavy Lifting

This isn't a manual savings tracker. The AI layer makes it genuinely useful:

  • Goal suggestions — based on spend history, travel seasonality, and coin burn rate, the AI recommends a trip you could realistically fund in 3–6 months
  • Dynamic pricing awareness — pulls live flight + hotel data for the goal destination and updates the coin target as prices move
  • Spend optimisation nudges — identifies upcoming spend moments (rent, groceries, dining) where the user isn't maximising coin earn, and prompts the switch
  • Goal completion celebration — when the user hits their coin target, the app surfaces a one-tap booking flow for that exact trip, pre-filled with coins applied

How I'd Measure Success

North Star
Goals-to-bookings conversion rate
Did setting a goal actually change redemption behaviour? This is the only metric that proves the feature isn't just a dashboard.
Leading Indicator
Weekly active users on goal card
Are users checking in? If the goal card has high session frequency, it's working as a habit hook — the booking will follow.
Guardrail
Coin accumulation rate (non-nudged vs nudged cohort)
Are nudges actually accelerating spend, or just annoying users? If nudged cohort accumulates meaningfully faster without unsubscribing, the AI is earning its keep.
Business Metric
In-app booking GMV from goal completions
Scapia earns on booking margins. If goal completions drive booking, the feature pays for itself directly.
